📖 The Lesson
Three scenarios. Which organ is at work?
⏱ 15 min
👁️
Section 1
What Is an Organ?

An organ is a body part that does a specific job. Each organ has one main FUNCTION (or role). The heart's function is to pump blood. The lungs' function is to handle breathing. The brain's function is to control everything else.

You have over 70 organs in your body. Some are huge (like skin — your biggest organ). Some are tiny (like the pancreas). Some you can't live without (like the heart and brain). Others you can survive without (like the appendix).

🤔
Stop and Think
"What's the difference between an ORGAN (like the heart) and a BODY PART (like a finger)? Why does the heart count and a finger might not?"
🧾
Section 2
Why Evidence Matters

EvidenceevidenceObservations or data that support or test an idea. is what scientists use to back up their ideas. The more careful your observations, the stronger your evidence becomes. A scientist doesn't say, "I think the leaves are turning yellow." They write down the dates, the colors, and the counts — so anyone could check.

If one organ stops working, the others quickly run into trouble. The brain depends on the heart for oxygen. The heart depends on the lungs for that oxygen in the first place. Every part of you depends on every other part.

🧪
Stop and Reason
"Imagine two friends both say a plant is dying. One says, 'It just looks sad.' The other says, 'Six of its eight leaves are brown, and the soil has been dry for five days.' Whose claim is stronger, and why?"
❓
Section 3
Asking Testable Questions

Scientists don't ask just any question — they ask testable questionstestable questionA question you can answer by observing, measuring, or testing.. "Why are clouds beautiful?" isn't testable. But "Do clouds form more often in the morning or the afternoon?" — that's something you could actually find out by watching the sky for a week.

Real-World Connection: When you exercise, almost every major organ works harder. Your heart beats faster. Your lungs breathe deeper. Your muscles use more energy. Your brain monitors it all. Even your skin sweats to cool you down. That's the body's team in action.

🔑 Key Concept

An organ is a body part with a specific job (function). Major human organs include the brain, heart, lungs, stomach, liver, kidneys, skin, and skeleton. Organs work together as teams — none acts alone.

Mind-Blowing Fact
Your heart beats about 100,000 times every day — without you ever telling it to. In an average lifetime, that's about 2.5 BILLION beats. It pumps the equivalent of 2,000 gallons of blood through your body every single day.
Go Deeper — How many organs do you actually have?

Scientists count around 78 major organs in the human body, plus dozens more if you count smaller structures. Many work in teams called SYSTEMS — like the digestive system (mouth, stomach, intestines, liver, pancreas) or the circulatory system (heart, blood vessels, blood).

If you visited a research station today, you'd probably find shelves full of yellow waterproof notebooks. Some are 30 or 40 years old, still being checked when scientists need to compare today's data to data from a long time ago.

Self-Check
Quick Self-Check
Not graded — just for you! See how much you understand.
Which organ controls everything else in your body?
AThe heart
BThe brain
CThe stomach
✅ Correct! The brain is your control center. It runs your thoughts AND your automatic stuff (heartbeat, breathing) without you having to think about it.
❌ Not quite. The heart pumps blood. The stomach digests food. What organ is the "boss" telling them when to work?
What is the function of the lungs?
APull in oxygen and push out carbon dioxide
BPump blood around the body
CBreak down food
✅ Correct! The lungs handle breathing. They grab oxygen from each breath in and send out CO₂ with each breath out.
❌ Not quite. Pumping blood is the heart's job. Breaking down food is the stomach's. What organ do you use every time you breathe?
When you run upstairs, your heart and lungs both work harder. Why?
AThey were getting tired and needed a break
BThey aren't connected — they just happen to both speed up
CYour muscles need more oxygen, so the lungs and heart work together to deliver it
✅ Correct! Organs work as a team. Lungs bring in extra oxygen. The heart pumps it to your muscles. The muscles use it to move you. Beautiful coordination.
❌ Not quite. Organs working harder isn't about getting tired. And they ARE connected — through your blood. What do running muscles need more of?
