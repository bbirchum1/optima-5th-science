📖 The Lesson
Energy comes in six familiar forms
⏱ 15 min
🛡️
Section 1
What Is Energy?

Energy is the ability to cause motion or change. That's the short definition. The long version: anything that gets something else to move, warm up, light up, vibrate, or change shape is energy in action.

You can't hold energy in your hand the way you can hold a ball. But you can see what energy does. A car moving down the road has energy. A ringing bell has energy. A warm cup of tea has energy. Each one is using a different form.

🤔
Stop and Think
"What's one thing in your room right now that's moving, warming, glowing, or making a sound? What kind of energy is at work?"
✂️
Section 2
Six Forms of Energy

Scientists usually group everyday energy into six big forms. Light energy lets you see. Heat energy makes things warm. Sound energy lets you hear. Electrical energy flows through wires. Chemical energy is stored in food, fuel, and batteries. Mechanical energy is the energy of things in motion or held up high.

Most everyday things use more than one form at once. A flashlight gives off light AND a little heat. A blender makes sound AND uses electrical energy. A campfire makes light AND heat AND sound (cracking and popping). Once you start looking, you'll see energy in dozens of places at once.

🧪
Stop and Reason
"Pick one room in your house. Name at least three different forms of energy you could see, hear, or feel there right now."
📦
Section 3
Energy Causes Motion or Change

The thing all six forms of energy have in common: they make something else move or change. Light energy makes a plant grow. Heat energy makes water boil. Sound energy makes your eardrum vibrate. Mechanical energy moves a soccer ball. Electrical energy makes a fan spin. Chemical energy makes your muscles work.

Energy can also change FROM one form INTO another. The sun's light becomes a plant's chemical energy. A battery's chemical energy becomes the electrical energy that makes a flashlight glow. We'll explore those transformations in our next module.

Real-World Connection: Think about your phone. It uses electrical energy from the battery. The screen makes light energy. The speaker makes sound energy. Vibrating notifications use mechanical energy. The battery itself stores chemical energy. One small device — five forms.

🔑 Key Concept

Energy is the ability to cause motion or change. The six main forms are light, heat, sound, electrical, chemical, and mechanical. Each form looks different, but they all do the same job: they make things happen.

Mind-Blowing Fact
Your body uses about 2,000 food calories of chemical energy every single day — even when you're sleeping! That's about the same as 50 cups of hot tea worth of energy. Where does it go? Most of it becomes heat. The rest powers your muscles, your brain, and every breath you take.
Go Deeper — What about nuclear energy?

There's a seventh form of energy: nuclear energy. It's stored inside the tiny center (called the nucleus) of every atom. The sun is powered by nuclear energy — it's what makes the sun shine. On Earth, we use nuclear energy in power plants to make electricity.

Nuclear energy is incredibly powerful — a tiny amount can do a huge job. But it can also be dangerous, which is why scientists who work with it use heavy safety equipment. For everyday life, the six forms you learned about today are plenty.

Self-Check
Quick Self-Check
Not graded — just for you! See how much you understand.
Which of these is an example of SOUND energy at work?
AA flashlight beam in a dark room
BA hot mug of cocoa
CA drummer hitting a snare drum
✅ Correct! When the drum is hit, it vibrates. Those vibrations travel through the air as sound. Your ear picks them up — that's sound energy.
❌ Not quite. The flashlight is using light energy. The hot cocoa shows heat energy. Sound energy is something you HEAR — vibrations traveling through the air.
Where is CHEMICAL energy STORED?
AInside a moving soccer ball
BInside food, fuel, and batteries
CInside a beam of sunlight
✅ Correct! Chemical energy is stored in the bonds between particles in food, fuel, and batteries. When the bonds break, the energy is released.
❌ Not quite. A moving soccer ball has mechanical energy. Sunlight is light energy. Chemical energy is the stored kind — locked inside something until it's used.
A blender turns on and chops up some fruit. Which forms of energy are at work?
AElectrical (to power it), sound (the buzz), and mechanical (the blades spinning)
BOnly electrical energy
COnly mechanical energy
✅ Correct! Most appliances use more than one form of energy at once. The blender draws electrical energy, makes sound, and uses mechanical energy to spin the blades.
❌ Not quite. The blender uses electrical energy to power up, but it also makes sound (you can hear it!) and uses mechanical energy (spinning blades). Look for ALL the forms at once.
